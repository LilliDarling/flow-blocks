Your Energy Patterns

Based on the last 14 days of check-ins

🌱 Energy-first, not time-first

You don't run on a clock — you run on energy and interest. Your pool is full of things you could do. Check in with how you're feeling, and the app highlights what fits. No pressure to decide in advance what goes where.

🚪 Transition buffers are airlocks

Switching gears takes time — more than you think. Those 10-15 minute buffers between pinned blocks aren't wasted time; they're the space you need to let go of one thing and gear up for the next. Protect them fiercely.

🍽️ The menu approach

Give yourself 2-3 task options per card. "Push: tackle report OR deep-clean kitchen OR intense workout." Having choices gives you autonomy and novelty, which makes engagement way more likely than a single rigid assignment.

⚓ Pin only what truly needs a time

Meetings, appointments, time-sensitive things — those get pinned. Everything else lives in your pool. The fewer rigid commitments, the less guilt when things shift. And they will shift. That's fine.

✅ Track what you DID, not just what you planned

We tend to undercount our own output. Mark things as you go — with the time and how long they took — and they land on your week view at the moment you did them. Pinned or not, it's the same: what actually happened, not what was on the plan.

🔋 Low energy is data, not a flaw

Some days your battery is at 30%. That's information, not a character flaw. On low-energy days, your pool defaults to drift and rest options — everything else stays one tap away under "the rest of your pool." Trying to force a push when you're depleted just creates shame spirals. Respect the battery level.

🌿 Growth isn't always productive

Journaling, therapy, learning, reflecting — these are growth blocks. They don't produce a deliverable, but they're some of the most important work you'll do. Give them space in your day without guilt.

🌀 Going off-script is a feature

Your day went sideways? That's not failure — that's life. Hit the "day went sideways" button and your remaining pinned blocks move back to your pool. There's no "ruined plan" — just a different day. Pick what fits the one you're actually having.

🔍 Patterns show up over time

As you complete, skip, and check in with your energy, Wildbloom quietly watches for patterns — which block types you finish when your energy is high, which days you tend to skip a particular type, whether a streak is building. When it sees enough signal, it surfaces a gentle observation on your day view. They're noticings, not prescriptions — keep what's useful, ignore what isn't.

Your Data & Privacy

Wildbloom stores your data securely in the cloud so it syncs across devices. Here's what we keep and for how long:

  • Recurring blocks & settings — kept as long as your account exists. These are your templates.
  • One-off blocks — automatically removed 7 days after their scheduled date.
  • Daily completions (done/skipped status) — kept for 7 days, then cleaned up.
  • Done list items — kept for 30 days.
  • Energy logs — kept for 30 days (14 days are used for your analytics chart).
  • Pomodoro sessions — kept for 30 days.
  • Reminders — kept as long as your account exists. Daily completion history kept for 30 days.
  • Event log — every action (completions, skips, energy check-ins) is recorded as a lightweight event. This is what powers the insights on your day view — streaks, energy-type correlations, skip patterns. Kept for 60 days, which gives weekly patterns enough repetitions to surface confidently, then cleaned up. Learned patterns persist in a separate summary so your insights don't reset.

Cleanup runs automatically each night. Insights are computed from your own events only — nothing leaves your account and no model is trained across users. All data is protected by row-level security — only you can see your own data. We never share or sell your information.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 27, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you create an account, we collect your email address and authentication credentials. As you use Wildbloom, we store the data you create: time blocks, energy logs, pomodoro sessions, reminders, done-list items, and calendar connections.

2. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used solely to provide and improve the Wildbloom service. We use it to sync your schedule across devices, generate your energy analytics, send reminders you configure, and display your calendar events. We do not use your data for advertising or profiling.

3. Data Storage & Security

All data is stored in Supabase with row-level security enabled, meaning only you can access your own data. Connections are encrypted in transit via TLS. Calendar OAuth tokens are stored encrypted and are only used to fetch your calendar events.

4. Data Retention

We retain data only as long as needed:

  • Recurring blocks, settings, and reminders are kept as long as your account exists.
  • One-off blocks are removed 7 days after their scheduled date.
  • Daily completions are kept for 7 days.
  • Done-list items, energy logs, and pomodoro sessions are kept for 30 days.
  • Reminder completion history is kept for 30 days.

Automated cleanup runs nightly.

5. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and edge functions.
  • Google Calendar API — only when you connect your calendar, and only to read event data.

We do not share, sell, or rent your personal data to any other third parties.

6. Your Rights

You can export or delete your data at any time by contacting us. Deleting your account removes all associated data from our systems.

7. Cookies

Wildbloom does not use tracking cookies or analytics scripts. We use local storage only for theme preference and session management.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through the app. Continued use of Wildbloom after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, contact us at lillith@valkyrieremedy.com.
